# Billing vs Technical Routing

## Issue
Customer concern may belong either to billing support or technical support, and correct routing is needed.

## Symptoms
- Customer mentions pricing, plan changes, or invoices
- Customer mentions speed, outage, hardware, or service instability
- Customer mixes technical and billing concerns in the same conversation

## Diagnosis
1. Identify whether the main problem is about payment, pricing, contract, or invoice details.
2. Identify whether the main problem is about service quality, connectivity, equipment, or outages.
3. If both are present, determine which issue blocks the customer most urgently.

## Recommended Agent Action
- Route pricing, invoicing, and package disputes to billing support.
- Route outages, slow speeds, or hardware issues to technical support.
- If the issue spans both areas, handle the immediate technical diagnosis first and then redirect billing questions when needed.

## Escalation Criteria
- Customer has both unresolved billing and unresolved technical issues
- Previous team transferred the case incorrectly
- Cross-team handoff is needed to avoid repeated customer contact

## Suggested Reply
I want to make sure this is handled by the right team. If the main issue is related to pricing, billing, or your package, I’ll route this to billing support. If the main issue is about service quality or connectivity, I’ll continue with technical troubleshooting first.
