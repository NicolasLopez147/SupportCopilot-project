# Local Outage Check

## Issue
Customer reports loss of service or degraded service that may be linked to a local outage.

## Symptoms
- No internet access
- Multiple customers in the same area may be affected
- Service interruption started suddenly
- Equipment restart did not restore service

## Diagnosis
1. Check whether there is an open outage or maintenance event in the customer area.
2. Confirm the time when the service interruption started.
3. Ask whether the customer has already restarted the equipment.
4. If an outage exists, verify whether an estimated resolution time is available.

## Recommended Agent Action
- Inform the customer if a local outage is already identified.
- Avoid unnecessary troubleshooting if the outage clearly explains the issue.
- If no outage is found, continue with standard technical diagnosis.

## Escalation Criteria
- No outage is found but the issue affects multiple households
- The outage lasts significantly longer than expected
- Service does not return after the outage is marked as resolved

## Suggested Reply
I’m checking whether there is a local outage currently affecting your area. If there is an ongoing incident, I’ll share the available status with you. If no outage is detected, I’ll continue with technical troubleshooting to identify the next step.
