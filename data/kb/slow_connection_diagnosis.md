# Slow Connection Diagnosis

## Issue
Customer reports that the internet connection is available but significantly slower than expected.

## Symptoms
- Pages load slowly
- Video streaming buffers frequently
- Downloads take much longer than usual
- Problem may occur on one or multiple devices

## Diagnosis
1. Ask whether the slowdown affects Wi-Fi only or both Wi-Fi and wired devices.
2. Ask whether the problem is constant or only happens at certain times of day.
3. Confirm whether the customer restarted the router recently.
4. Check for known incidents, maintenance, or congestion in the area.
5. If possible, ask for a quick speed test result.

## Recommended Agent Action
- Distinguish between a local Wi-Fi issue and a broader line issue.
- If the slowdown affects all devices, check outage and line status first.
- If the slowdown is limited to Wi-Fi, guide the customer through router placement and reboot checks.
- If the issue persists with no outage detected, create a technical diagnostic request.

## Escalation Criteria
- Slow speeds affect all devices
- Wired connection is also degraded
- Problem persists after reboot
- Speed remains far below the subscribed service level

## Suggested Reply
Thanks for the information. I’d like to confirm whether the slowdown affects all devices or only Wi-Fi, and whether you already restarted the router. I will also check whether there is a known issue affecting your area before deciding whether deeper technical diagnostics are needed.
