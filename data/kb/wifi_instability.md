# WiFi Instability

## Issue
Customer reports that the Wi-Fi connection drops, becomes unstable, or works inconsistently across devices.

## Symptoms
- Wi-Fi disconnects frequently
- Some rooms have weak signal
- Connection works on one device but fails on another
- Service may be stable on wired connection but unstable on Wi-Fi

## Diagnosis
1. Ask whether the issue affects Wi-Fi only or also wired devices.
2. Check whether the instability happens in all rooms or only in certain areas.
3. Ask where the router is located and whether there are obstacles nearby.
4. Confirm whether the router was recently restarted.

## Recommended Agent Action
- Distinguish Wi-Fi instability from a wider service outage.
- If the issue is limited to Wi-Fi, guide the customer through router placement and reboot checks.
- If the issue affects all connection types, continue with outage and line diagnosis.

## Escalation Criteria
- Wi-Fi remains unstable after reboot and placement checks
- Multiple devices are affected in all rooms
- Issue appears to be linked to hardware fault

## Suggested Reply
I’d like to check whether the instability affects only Wi-Fi or the whole connection. If it is limited to Wi-Fi, we can first review router placement and restart the equipment before moving to a deeper technical check.
